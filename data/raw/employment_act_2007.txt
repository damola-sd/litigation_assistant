EMPLOYMENT ACT
NO. 11 OF 2007
LAWS OF KENYA

PART I — PRELIMINARY

1. Short title and commencement
This Act may be cited as the Employment Act, 2007.

2. Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:
"contract of service" means an agreement, whether oral or written, express or implied, to employ
or to serve as an employee for a period of time and includes a contract of apprenticeship and
indentured learnership.
"employee" means a person employed for wages or a salary and includes a casual employee.
"employer" means any person, public body, firm, corporation or company who or which has entered
into a contract of service with an employee.
"wages" means all remuneration, including allowances, paid or payable to an employee in respect
of work done or to be done.

PART II — FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS

3. Prohibition of forced labour
No person shall be required to perform forced or compulsory labour.

4. Prohibition of discrimination
(1) An employer shall not discriminate directly or indirectly against an employee or prospective
employee on grounds of race, colour, sex, language, religion, political or other opinion,
nationality, ethnic or social origin, disability, pregnancy, mental status, or HIV status.

(2) Any employment policy, criteria or practice that is apparently neutral but in practice
disadvantages employees on a prohibited ground is indirect discrimination unless it can be
justified on objective grounds.

PART III — EMPLOYMENT CONTRACTS

7. Form of contract
(1) An employment contract may be oral or in writing, but a written statement of particulars
must be provided to employees working for more than one month.

(2) The written statement of particulars shall include:
    (a) the name and address of the employer;
    (b) the name of the employee and their job title;
    (c) the date of commencement of employment;
    (d) the place of work;
    (e) the wages or salary;
    (f) the hours of work;
    (g) the leave entitlement;
    (h) the notice period required to terminate the contract.

8. Probationary period
(1) A contract of service may include a probationary period not exceeding six months.

(2) During the probationary period, either party may terminate the contract on not less than
seven days' notice or payment in lieu of notice.

(3) A probationary period may be extended by mutual agreement but the total probationary period
shall not exceed twelve months.

PART IV — TERMINATION AND DISMISSAL

41. Summary dismissal
(1) An employer is entitled to summarily dismiss an employee without notice or payment in lieu
of notice if the employee:
    (a) wilfully disobeys a lawful and reasonable order of the employer;
    (b) is guilty of gross misconduct;
    (c) is absent from work without permission or reasonable cause;
    (d) is guilty of a criminal offence related to employment.

(2) Before summarily dismissing an employee, the employer shall:
    (a) inform the employee of the reasons for the intended dismissal;
    (b) give the employee an opportunity to respond to the allegations against them;
    (c) genuinely consider the employee's response before making the decision.

42. Termination on notice
(1) Either party to a contract of service may terminate the contract by giving notice in writing
to the other party.

(2) The minimum notice periods are:
    (a) where the employee has been employed for a period not exceeding two months — twenty-eight days;
    (b) where the employee has been employed for two months or more — thirty days.

(3) An employer may, instead of giving notice, pay the employee the wages the employee would have
received during the notice period (payment in lieu of notice).

43. Valid reason for termination
(1) An employer shall not terminate a contract of service unless there is valid reason for the
termination connected with the employee's capacity, conduct, or compatibility, or based on the
operational requirements of the employer.

(2) The employer bears the burden of proving the valid reason for termination.

(3) Where there is no valid reason for termination, or where the procedural requirements have
not been followed, the termination is unfair.

44. Unfair termination
(1) A termination is unfair if the employer fails to prove:
    (a) that there was a valid reason for the termination; and
    (b) that the employee was notified of the reason and given an opportunity to respond.

(2) Where an employee is unfairly terminated, the Industrial Court may order reinstatement,
re-engagement, or compensation.

(3) Compensation for unfair dismissal shall not exceed twelve months' gross wages.

45. Redundancy
(1) An employer may terminate an employee's contract by reason of redundancy where:
    (a) the employer is reorganising the enterprise;
    (b) the employer is reducing the work force;
    (c) the position has been eliminated.

(2) Before declaring redundancy, the employer shall notify the employee and the relevant union
(if any) and attempt to avoid or minimize redundancies.

(3) A redundant employee is entitled to severance pay of not less than fifteen days' pay for each
completed year of service.

PART V — WAGES AND REMUNERATION

48. Payment of wages
(1) An employer shall pay an employee their wages in full on the agreed payment date.

(2) An employer shall not make deductions from wages except in the prescribed circumstances.

(3) Wages are a debt owed by the employer to the employee and are recoverable in court.

PART VI — LEAVE

28. Annual leave
(1) An employee who has worked for a continuous period of not less than one year is entitled
to not less than twenty-one working days of annual leave with full pay.

(2) Leave shall be granted at the time agreed between the employer and the employee.

29. Sick leave
(1) An employee is entitled to sick leave with full pay for not less than seven days, and
thereafter to sick leave with half pay for not less than seven days in each period of twelve
consecutive months of service.

PART VII — REMEDIES AND ENFORCEMENT

76. Remedies for breach of employment contract
(1) An employee who has suffered a breach of their employment contract may apply to the
Employment and Labour Relations Court for:
    (a) a declaration that the breach has occurred;
    (b) reinstatement or re-engagement;
    (c) compensation for damages suffered;
    (d) payment of outstanding wages and benefits.

(2) The Employment and Labour Relations Court has exclusive jurisdiction over employment disputes.

77. Limitation period
Claims under this Act must be brought within three years from the date the cause of action arose.
